1. The Challenge

Managing chronic illness is a full-time job that involves complex medication schedules, frequent specialist consultations, and constant monitoring of health data. The CareFlow app is designed to alleviate the "cognitive load" placed on patients.

The Problem

Patients often struggle with medication adherence (forgetting doses) and fragmented communication between different doctors and specialists.

The Solution

A unified mobile platform that integrates medication reminders, vital tracking, and direct provider communication into one cohesive ecosystem.

2. Research Summary

To validate our approach, we conducted a survey of 12 participants aged 35-75 who manage at least two chronic conditions. Understanding their daily struggles was vital to forming a compassionate design strategy.

"65% of participants admitted to missing at least one dose per week."

Qualitative Insights

  • Users feel overwhelmed by managing multiple disjointed health portals.
  • Anxiety surrounding missed medications is a primary emotional pain point.
  • Tracking vitals is seen as a chore unless there is immediate feedback.

3. User Personas

Based on our research, we developed two distinct personas to guide our design decisions and feature prioritization.

S

"Systematic Sarah"

The Organized Tracker

Sarah relies heavily on her midday med checks. She needs a system that fits seamlessly into her busy workday without causing disruption. Her main goal is efficiency and maintaining her strict schedule.

S

"Senior Samuel"

The Vitals Monitor

Samuel needs simple, large interfaces to track his vitals. He values clear communication with his doctors and reassurance that his readings are within normal ranges.

9. Usability Testing Plan

Goals

  • Measure the time taken to mark a medication as "Taken."
  • Evaluate the discoverability of the Lab Results section.
  • Assess the user's emotional reaction to the "Normal Range" feedback loops.

Task List for Participants

  1. Sign in and identify when your next dose of Lisinopril is due.
  2. Log a heart rate of 75 bpm.
  3. Send a message to Dr. Chen asking about your lab results.

Feedback Analysis

We utilize SUS (System Usability Scale) scoring. Feedback is categorized into "Critical Blockers," "Experience Friction," and "Delighters." We iterate by simplifying the vitals entry form if users take longer than 15 seconds to log a value.

10. Reflections

The UX design process revealed that feedback is just as important as tracking. Initially, we simply stored data. Through interviews, we realized that seeing "Normal" or "Goal Met" provided the emotional incentive needed for chronic patients to keep using the app.

Overcoming Challenges

Balancing a "medical" feel (trustworthy) with a "lifestyle" feel (not depressing) was tough. We overcame this by using a soft teal and white palette rather than sterile hospital blues and greys.
